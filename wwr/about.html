History

Down the River has a rich history that dates back to the early 19th century. The sport originated in the United States, where explorers and adventurers would navigate the country's rivers in search of new lands and resources. The first recorded instance of down the river took place in 1811, when a group of fur traders used canoes to navigate the rapids of the Missouri River. In the early 20th century, white water rafting began to gain popularity as a recreational activity.The first commercial rafting trips were offered in the 1950s, and by the 1970s, the sport had become a popular way for outdoor enthusiasts to experience the thrill of navigating rapids and exploring remote wilderness areasToday, Down the River is enjoyed by millions of people around the world. It has evolved into a highly organized and regulated sport, with professional guides and safety protocols in place to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for all participants. Whether you're looking for a thrilling adventure or a peaceful escape into nature, Down the River offers something for everyone.
